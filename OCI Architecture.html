Module 1: OCI Core Architecture

The fundamental physical and logical building blocks of OCI, crucial for designing resilient applications.

Core Architectural Concepts

Region: A localized geographic area (e.g., Ashburn). Consists of one or more ADs and is isolated from other regions.

Availability Domain (AD): A fault-tolerant, separate data center within a Region. Has its own power, cooling, and network.

Fault Domain (FD): A grouping of hardware within an AD (like a server rack). Each AD has three FDs for anti-affinity.

How to Choose the Right OCI Region

1. Latency & Performance: Choose the region closest to your users.

2. Data Residency & Compliance: Adhere to local data storage laws (often overrides latency).

3. Service Availability: Verify needed OCI services are in the chosen region.

Designing for High Availability (HA)

Level 1 (Within an AD): Deploy redundant components across different Fault Domains.

Level 2 (Across ADs): Replicate your entire setup across multiple Availability Domains for maximum availability and disaster recovery.
